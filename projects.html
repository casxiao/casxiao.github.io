"So, You've Discovered Sleeves!": The Reinforcement of Hypersexualized Gender Norms in Resident Evil Remakes

This study examines how Resident Evil remakes have redesigned female characters’ outfits via textual analysis through a feminist lens. Hypersexualization by visual cues, such as the amount of exposed skin, exaggerated body parts, and visible cleavage, were considered through iterative comparison of the original and remade designs.

Upon determining the initial level of hypersexualization, the study questioned how the design's amendments attributed to an emphasis on the perceived gender norms and female stereotypes in the original titles. Evidence determined a reduction of hypersexualized gender norms in remakes, and an evident gap in hypersexualization between original and remade designs.

This study was individually written and conducted for STCM244 Research, Measurement, and Evaluation.

Restore. Rebuild. Reunite. | Advertising & PR Portfolio for ReConnect Rondo

ReConnect Rondo, an organization dedicated to rebuilding the Rondo Community lost to the construction of I-95, connected with STCM344 in sought of advertising and public relation portfolios.
