"So, You've Discovered Sleeves!": The Reinforcement of Hypersexualized Gender Norms in Resident Evil Remakes

This study examines how Resident Evil remakes have redesigned female characters’ outfits via textual analysis through a feminist lens. Hypersexualization by visual cues, such as the amount of exposed skin, exaggerated body parts, and visible cleavage, were considered through iterative comparison of the original and remade designs.

Upon determining the initial level of hypersexualization, the study questioned how the design's amendments attributed to an emphasis on the perceived gender norms and female stereotypes in the original titles. Evidence determined a reduction of hypersexualized gender norms in remakes, and an evident gap in hypersexualization between original and remade designs.

This study was individually written and conducted for STCM244 Research, Measurement, and Evaluation.

"Sample to Insight" Infographic for the MWMO.

The Mississippi Watershed Management Organization teamed with Emerging Media students amidst their full rebrand (i.e. website, logo, graphics, etc.). The MWMO focuses on collecting and analyzing water samples from a storm drain or river to provide quality data for the Greater Minneapolis-St. Paul area.

For this project, I was a member of the design team, more specifically the infographic team. This infographic was created via Adobe Illustrator over the course of about 6 weeks, and displays an illustration of the general process of retrieving a water sample and turning it into an insight that can be shared with the community. The client prioritized minimal design and color, as well as minimal text descriptions, for this infographic. The design is the final product of balancing client needs with a communicative design that can be comprehended easily by non-scientists.

This design was individually created, with peer and client feedback, for DIMA480 Digital Media for the Common Good.
