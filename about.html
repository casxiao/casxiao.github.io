"So, You've Discovered Sleeves!": The Reinforcement of Hypersexualized Gender Norms in Resident Evil Remakes"

This study examines how Resident Evil remakes have redesigned female characters’ outfits via textual analysis through a feminist lens. Hypersexualization by visual cues, such as the amount of exposed skin, exaggerated body parts, and visible cleavage, were considered through iterative comparison of the original and remade designs. Upon determining the initial level of hypersexualization led to questioning of how the design's amendments attributed to an emphasis on the perceived gender norms and female stereotypes in the original titles. Evidence determined a reduction of hypersexualized gender norms in remakes, and an evident gap in hypersexualization between original and remade designs. This study was individually written and conducted for STCM244 Research, Measurement, and Evaluation.
